## What a virtual pattern is

A **virtual pattern** is a pattern the build *names or implies* but that does **not
exist as a formalized `pattern/library` node** — the true "induced hole" (distinct
from the cluster-grain hole-basins, and from `:try-candidates` that DO resolve). The
magnet's embedding neighborhood keeps proposing these names across missions; they are
the cascade's own suggestion of *what to seed next*.

Concretely: over `futon6/data/mission-pattern-scopes.edn` `:try-candidates`, resolved
against the live `pattern/library`, **38 distinct names are referenced but absent**,
across **183 mission-citations**. (The `:applied` layer has **zero** virtual — confirmed
patterns are all formalized; virtuals live only in the magnet's *suggestions*.) The same
names re-surface from the live on-demand magnet (`cascade_construct`) — e.g. the
autoclock-in refresh induced `interest-event-vocabulary` + `read-existing-seam-before-implementing`.

## Where they live (and why NOT as bare holes)

**They are the A×B join** — the point where Checklist B (the cascade render) hands back
to Checklist A (the R16 fold / impl #3). Per [[project-virtual-patterns-land-with-aif]],
a virtual pattern must land **with fold ΔG-backing**, NOT as a bare `cascade/hole`: the
fold is precisely the machinery that turns a proposed pattern into a **construction +
ΔG** (a typed-hole and its filling — [[project-typed-holes-mission]]). Landing them as
grounded-looking holes would launder folklore; recording them as a *named, queryable
candidate inventory awaiting backing* is honest.

So the home is this excursion + `virtual-patterns.edn` (the queryable candidate set),
**referenced from the AIF board** (`futon2/holes/aif-wiring-explainer.html`, the R16-fold
foliation, sibling to `E-library-coverage`). When impl #3 (the embedding fold) or #2
(LLM-turn) runs, each virtual pattern is a fold TARGET: construct its wiring, score its
ΔG, and — if it discharges — promote it from virtual → formalized `pattern/library`.
