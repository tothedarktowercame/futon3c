## The loop (super simple — keep it that way)

1. **Half-baked pudding** → a `:held` capability (the star-map / pudding-prover lists them).
2. **Short list** → that capability's `:minted-by` + graph-neighbour missions. A handful.
3. **A pattern that warrants evolving one** → search the futon3 library (853 flexiargs, hotword index) for a
   pattern whose warrant fits a mission's current lifecycle state.
4. **Apply it + record a PUR** → evolve the mission one step; the PUR is the honest evaluation (incl.
   "didn't help" = a real negative result — *that is still progress: information gained*).
5. **Commit to a branch. Done.** Turn done, progress made, on a branch, with Joe's imprimatur in advance.

## Strategic, NOT random — the loop persists toward fruit (Joe, 2026-06-08)

The loop is **not** "try → pan out, or demote the pattern and reroll at random." That's flitting, and it rarely
fruits. Each cycle is a **move in a strategy** to **clear a star-map level** (attest a `:held` capability) or
**earn a validated peradam** — the moves *accumulate* labor toward that fruit. A move that yields
**labor-but-no-fruit is a step, not a failure**: its PUR *informs the next move in the same strategy*; it does
**not** demote the pattern and reroll.

- **Binds explore to exploit.** The marking-rule (exploit) names *which level to clear*; the warranted tries
  (explore) are the *moves toward clearing it*. The tries are `G-info` **in service of the goal-marking**, not
  random uncertainty-reduction.
- **The virtue is persistence, not dāna-flitting.** Sustained, determined effort toward a level is 🟤 viriya /
  🔴 adhiṭṭhāna ([[M-pudding-peradams]] §3), holding the unresolved level (⚫ khanti) while it ripens.
  Random-retry is the *absence* of those orbs.
- **The certification gate holds throughout** (M-pudding-peradams §4): a move mints a certified peradam only when
  the **fruit-witness** lands (a proof advances / the level clears). So the *strategy's* job is to reach the fruit;
  individual moves accrue the labor.

*Example (cycle 1):* the staleness/supersession finding for `M-hypergraph-operator` is **move 1** of a strategy
to clear the `:hypergraph-operator` level — not a failed random try. The strategy continues: **verify the
supersession both-live → on that evidence, the operator's fold/close ruling → the level clears (fruit)**. We do
**not** demote `staleness-scan`; we persist along the trail it opened.

## How it composes (the two poles)

- A try that **pans out → graduates into a pre-witness** (explore feeds exploit; the marking-rule then drifts to it).
- A try that **doesn't → un-fired**, filed as an honest negative. Either way, *information gained*.
- This is Bayesian structure-learning on the WM's own capability graph ([[project_bayesian_structure_learning]]):
  warranted hypothesis → evaluate → promote or revoke.
